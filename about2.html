About NSBE GSU

Welcome to NSBE at Georgia Southern University!

The Georgia Southern University Chapter of the National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE GSU) was proudly revived during the 2023-2024 academic year after seven years of dormancy. Thanks to the dedication of seven passionate individuals (Elijah Phillips, Nancy Sarpong, Winnie Mwangi, Miles Rollins, Isaiah Lee, Amber Lawrence, and Reasun Allah-U-Akbar) our chapter is back and stronger than ever, committed to empowering and uplifting members in STEM.

At NSBE GSU, we provide academic support through study sessions, professional development with skill-building workshops, and social connections via networking events. As a member, you’ll also gain access to exclusive opportunities like the Fall Regional Conference (FRC) and the Annual National Convention perfect platforms for expanding your network and enhancing your career prospects.

Join GSU NSBE today and become part of a vibrant community that fosters excellence, achievement, and unity in STEM. Together, we’re shaping the future!

About NSBE

The National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE) was founded in 1975 by six students at Purdue University in West Lafayette, Indiana. The students, who were all black engineering students, were concerned about the low number of black engineers in the workforce and the high dropout rate of black engineering students. They decided to form a student organization to address these issues.

The first chapter of NSBE was called the Society of Black Engineers (BSE). The BSE quickly grew in popularity and soon chapters were established at other universities across the country. In 1976, the BSE was renamed the National Society of Black Engineers and incorporated as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization.

Today, NSBE is one of the largest student-run organizations in the United States. It has over 600 chapters and more than 24,000 members in the United States and abroad.
